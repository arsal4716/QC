# 1. Sales
*Sub-Dispositions:*
- *CX Sale* – This disposition applies ONLY when the enrollment, purchase, or plan transfer is fully completed within the call AND confirmed verbally by the Licensed Agent (LA) with explicit closing language.

### Required Conditions (ALL MUST BE PRESENT):
To classify a call as *Sales → CX Sale*, the following must ALL occur:

- **Full Qualification Sequence Completed**  
  - Age, residency, coverage details, income (if applicable), SSN (if required), and all campaign-specific fields have been verified with CX.

- **Explicit Consent from Customer**  
  - CX must verbally agree to proceed with the enrollment or transfer.  
  - Phrases like: “Yes, I agree,” “Yes, go ahead,” “Yes, enroll me,” “I authorize it.”

- **LA Provides Formal Enrollment/Submission Confirmation**  
  The LA MUST say one or more of these:  
  - “You are now enrolled.”  
  - “Your application has been submitted successfully.”  
  - “Your plan will start on [date].”  
  - “You will receive your card and welcome kit in 7–14 business days.”  
  - “Everything is finalized.”  
  - “All forms have been submitted.”

- **Closing Statements**  
  - LA wraps the call with a closing phrase:  
    *“Welcome to the plan,” “You’re all set,” “Congratulations,” “Your enrollment is complete.”*

### If ANY of the above is missing → DO NOT classify as Sales.

### **Critical Clarification (Updated & Strict):**
A call must **NEVER** be classified as Sales if:

- CX hangs up before confirmation  
- LA hangs up before confirmation  
- The call drops  
- CX is still answering qualification questions  
- CX only shows interest without consenting  
- LA schedules a callback or future follow-up  
- LA says “I will call you back to complete this”  
- CX says “Call me later,” “I’ll think about it,” “I’m busy now”  
- CX mentions active Medicare/other coverage that makes them NQ  
- CX does not verbally authorize enrollment  
- LA does not verbally confirm the enrollment submission  
- Enrollment was NOT fully finalized **inside the same call**

> **If there is ANY doubt, uncertainty, or incomplete information → DO NOT classify as Sales.**

- **LA hangup** → Target hung up → LA Hangup Call

- **LA schedules a future date** (“We’ll continue this Dec 11”) → Callback

*Sub-Dispositions:*
- *CX Disqualifies* - LA said "we don't offer that sorry" or you don't qualify. Use this when CX clearly does not meet campaign rules but no more specific sub-disposition fits (for example: complex medical/eligibility scenario, power of attorney issues, or mixed conditions).  

- *CX has Medicare* – **Highly restricted usage**:  
  - CX explicitly and clearly says they **have Medicare** (e.g., "I have Medicare", "I'm on Medicare Part A and B", "I’m on a Medicare plan").  
  - The campaign is **ACA** or **U65 Health Insurance**.  
  - Medicare (or Medicare + other coverage) is the **reason** they are not eligible for ACA/U65.  
  - This disposition must **never** be used for Medicaid-only, nursing home, power of attorney, or other coverage types.  
  - Do **not** apply this disposition in Medicare campaigns, as having Medicare usually indicates potential qualification in those campaigns.

- *CX has Medicaid only* – CX has **Medicaid-only** coverage (no Medicare) that disqualifies them from ACA/U65 or Medicare campaigns where Medicaid-only is not eligible.  
  - Key indicators: “I only have Medicaid”, “I’m just on Medicaid”, “I don’t have Medicare, only Medicaid.”

- *CX in Nursing Home* – CX lives in a **nursing home** or **long-term care facility** and this residential status disqualifies them based on campaign rules (e.g., some Medicare or insurance campaigns).  
  - Key indicators: “I live in a nursing home”, “I’m in a long-term care facility”, “I stay in a care home.”

- *CX Age NQ* - Age disqualification (***Only if age was explicitly discussed and found outside criteria***)  

- *CX can't afford plan* - Income too low (***must be explicitly mentioned***)  

- *CX Condition Not Met* - Not US citizen, residency issues, or other explicit eligibility conditions not covered above (e.g., VA/Tricare/employer conflicts when no better sub-disposition applies, power of attorney preventing direct enrollment if campaign requires direct CX consent).  

- *CX already has coverage* - Looking for other insurance types; CX is already covered in a way that conflicts with the campaign (but not strictly Medicare/Medicaid-only).  

- *CX Has Job* - Has employer-sponsored coverage  

  - *Key Phrases*: "I already had Medicare, Medicare, VA, Tricare", "Older than 65", "Less than 20k per year income", "Not US citizen", "Caller had employer-sponsored coverage", "I only have Medicaid", "I live in a nursing home / facility"

- *Not Qualified* = **strictly for confirmed eligibility failures** (age, coverage type, residency, income, coverage conflicts, nursing home, Medicaid-only, etc.).

# 9. Target hung up
*Sub-Dispositions:*
- *LA Hangup Call* – This disposition applies when the Licensed Agent (LA) is responsible for ending or abandoning the call, OR when no voice is detected from either party throughout the call. This includes any scenario where LA does not continue the call while CX was available or the system detects no actual conversation.

  - *Indicators* (any of these = Target Hung Up → LA Hangup Call):
  - **LA stops speaking mid-call**  
    LA suddenly stops talking, stops responding, or the audio from LA side drops while CX was still engaged or waiting.  
  - **LA disconnects while CX was still engaged**  
    If CX was responsive or available, and the call ends due to LA side termination.  
  - **Both sides are completely silent (dead air)**  
    If the audio contains no voice from LA or CX at any point (pure silence / empty audio), the call must be treated as LA hangup.  
  - **Only ringing is heard throughout the call**  
    If the call only rings with no voice detected from LA or CX, it is classified as LA hangup — LA never established the call connection.  
  - **LA does not introduce themselves or initiate speech**  
    If call connects but LA never speaks (no greeting, no attempt to start), the responsibility for the failed call is on LA.  
  - **System disconnect or auto-termination before CX interaction**  
    If the system drops the call before CX ever speaks AND LA never makes verbal contact, it is Target Hung Up.

  - *Summary Logic:*  
    **If LA ends the call, stops speaking, fails to speak at all, or if no audio from both sides exists — it is always Target hung up → LA Hangup Call.**

### Classification Priority Rules:
1. *Sales* takes absolute precedence if enrollment was completed with confirmation.  
   **→ Sales may ONLY be chosen if closing confirmation phrases or explicit consent are present. Partial progress, hangups, or incomplete flows must NOT be classified as Sales.**

1a. *Hangup and Silence Priority Rule*:
- If no voice is detected from either CX or LA for an extended period and the call ends, classify it under hangup logic only.  
- If CX hangs up or stops speaking mid-call → use **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**.  
- If LA stops speaking or terminates the call abruptly while CX is still engaged → use **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.  
- No other disposition (“CX has Medicare,” “Not Qualified,” etc.) may override hangup rules.  
- Silence = Hangup unless explicit technical issue is detected.  
- If CX becomes silent while LA continues speaking or the call audio cuts off from CX’s side, this is always treated as a CX hangup event, not a qualification or system issue.  
  Always classify such cases as **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**.  
- **Never choose any Not Qualified sub-disposition (including “CX has Medicare”) when the final call outcome is a hangup, silence, ring-only, or LA/CX disconnect. In such cases you must use the appropriate Hangup disposition.**

2. *DNC* classifications override all others when explicitly requested.

3. *Not Interested* vs *NQ*:  
   - **Use *Not Qualified* ONLY for explicit eligibility failures** (age, coverage type, residency, income, Medicaid-only, nursing home, etc.).  
   - **Use *Not Interested* for hangups, silences, or disengagements where no qualification failure is mentioned and outcome is driven by CX behavior.**  
   - **Never classify incomplete calls or mid-call hangups as Not Qualified.**

3a. ### Campaign-Specific Qualification Rule:

- In ACA, U65, if CX **explicitly mentions having Medicare (not Medicaid)**, classify as **Not Qualified → CX has Medicare**, because Medicare coverage disqualifies them from ACA/U65. For VA, Tricare, or employer coverage, use the most appropriate Not Qualified sub-disposition: **CX already has coverage**, **CX Has Job**, or **CX Condition Not Met**.  

- In Medicare Campaign, if CX mentions having Medicare, this should NOT be classified as Not Qualified — instead, treat it as a potentially Qualified lead (continue to verify Part A & B, Medicare card, and eligibility details). Medicare disqualification in Medicare campaigns should use **CX Disqualifies**, **CX Condition Not Met**, **CX has Medicaid only**, or **CX in Nursing Home**, as appropriate — **never “CX has Medicare” in Medicare campaigns.**  

4. *Callback* requires genuine interest + scheduling request.

5. *Unresponsive* vs *Tech Issues*: Use *Tech Issues* when audio problems are mentioned and *Unresponsive* for unintelligible responses from CX.

6. *Language barrier* only when communication completely fails due to language.

7. *Tech Issues* vs *Not Interested*: Use *Tech Issues* only when audio problems are explicitly mentioned ("can't hear you", "breaking up"), otherwise use *Not Interested* for call quality issues.

8. *Voicemail* vs *Not Interested*: Use *Voicemail* only when automated voicemail greeting is heard ("leave message after tone"), use *Not Interested* for human non-response.  

**8a. *IVR* vs *Voicemail*:**  
   - Classify as *IVR* if an automated system with interactive options is detected ("Press 1 for X", "For English, press 1").  
   - Classify as *Voicemail* if the system is a mailbox greeting ("Your call is very important to us", "Please leave your message after the tone").  
   - Never confuse IVR prompts with Voicemail greetings — IVR expects key presses, Voicemail expects a recorded message.

9. *Target hung up* vs *Not Interested (CX hangup)*:
Use *Target hung up → LA Hangup Call* ONLY when the **Licensed Agent (LA)** is the one who effectively ends or abandons the call. The following situations MUST always be classified as **Target hung up**:

- **No voice from BOTH sides (LA and CX)**  
   If the call contains only dead-air, silence, empty audio, or static with no speaking from either LA or CX,  
   → classify as **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.  
- **Only ringing, no voice ever appears**  
   If the call contains ringing only, with zero voice detected from LA or CX,  
   → classify as **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.  
   (This is considered LA hangup because LA never attempted a verbal connection.)  
- **LA stops speaking / disconnects while CX was still engaged or available**  
   If CX was previously speaking or responsive, but LA suddenly stops talking, disconnects, or fails to continue the call,  
   → classify as **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.  
- **Any scenario where LA terminates the call first**  
   If LA initiates call termination or abandons the conversation, this ALWAYS maps to  
   → **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.

Use *Not Interested → CX hangup Call* ONLY when **Customer (CX)** ends or abandons the call. The following situations MUST always be classified as **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**:

- **LA is speaking, asking a question, or actively guiding the call, but CX goes silent and audio stops**  
   If LA continues talking but no CX response is heard and the call ends,  
   → this is a CX hangup.  
- **CX disconnects mid-sentence or mid-conversation while LA is still engaged**  
   → classify as **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**.  
- **LA’s voice continues but CX audio suddenly disappears and the call drops**  
   This ALWAYS indicates customer hangup, not technical issue or qualification logic.  
- **Any situation where CX abandons the conversation first**  
   If CX cuts the call, stops responding, or drops the line while LA is still speaking,  
   → classify as **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**.

## FINAL DIFFERENTIATION LOGIC (1-sentence summary):
- **If LA ends the call, stops speaking, or if BOTH sides are silent or the call only rings → Target hung up (LA Hangup Call).**  
- **If LA is speaking and CX disappears, disconnects, or goes silent → Not Interested (CX hangup Call).**

10. *IVR* vs *Not Interested*: Use *IVR* only when automated system prompts are present ("Press 1 for X"), use *Not Interested* for human agent interactions.

11. *Misdialed* vs *Not Interested*: Use *Misdialed* only when wrong number is confirmed ("you have wrong number"), use *Not Interested* for general rejections.

12. *subsidy/incentivised* vs *NQ*: Use *subsidy/incentivised* only when CX already has coverage and seeks financial assistance, use *NQ* for coverage eligibility issues.


### Advanced Decision Logic:
*Outcome Hierarchy Rule*: When both a qualification signal (e.g., “has Medicare”) and a conversation outcome (e.g., hangup, refusal, call drop) occur in the same call, the conversation outcome takes priority.  
Classify hangups, refusals, and technical drops before evaluating qualification status.  
Example: If CX mentions Medicare early but hangs up mid-call, use **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**, not **Not Qualified → CX has Medicare**.

### CX Silence Detection Logic:
When LA is actively speaking but CX audio stops completely, treat this as a hangup event.  
Disposition should be **Not Interested → CX hangup Call**, regardless of earlier conversation content.

- *Implied Sales*: If agent says "We're all set" and customer says "Thanks, goodbye" = Sale  
- *Soft Rejections*: "I'll think about it" + immediate hangup = Not Interested  
- *Interest Indicators*: Questions about benefits, asking for clarification = potential positive outcome  
- *Frustration Escalation*: Track how tone changes throughout call  
- *Objection Handling*: Note if concerns were addressed and resolved
