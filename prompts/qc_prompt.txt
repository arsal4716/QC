## Global Priority Rules (Must Always Be Applied)

1. **Hangup Priority (Hard Rule)**

   * If **CX stops speaking / disconnects** while LA is still talking or trying to engage →
     **Disposition:** `Not Interested`
     **Sub-Disposition:** `CX hangup Call`

   * If **LA stops speaking / disconnects**, or **no voice at all** in the recording, or **only ringing** →
     **Disposition:** `Target hung up`
     **Sub-Disposition:** `LA Hangup Call`

   > Hangup / silence outcomes always override qualification outcomes (e.g., “CX has Medicare”).
   > Never use any *Not Qualified* sub-disposition if the final outcome is a hangup/silence.

2. **DNC Priority**
   If CX **explicitly** asks to stop calls, complain about too many calls, or requests DNC:
   → Always classify under **DNC**, never under Not Interested.

3. **Voicemail vs IVR vs Human**

   * Voicemail greeting at start → **Voicemail**
   * Automated system with menu / key-press options → **IVR**
   * No keys pressed or partial IVR → IVR sub-dispositions
   * Never mark Voicemail or IVR as Not Interested.

4. **Not Qualified vs Not Interested**

   * *Not Qualified* → use only when CX **explicitly fails eligibility criteria** (age, coverage type, income, residency, coverage conflicts, Medicaid-only, nursing home, etc.).
   * *Not Interested* → use when CX **refuses, hangs up, disengages, or declines** with no clear eligibility failure driving the outcome.

5. **Sales (Rarest Outcome)**

   * Only use **Sales** when the **entire enrollment / transfer process is fully completed** and clearly confirmed inside the same call.
   * If anything is incomplete, unclear, or deferred → **do NOT classify as Sales**.

6. **No Custom Labels**

   * You must **only** use the Dispositions and Sub-Dispositions defined below.
   * **No custom Dispositions, no custom Sub-Dispositions.**
   * Sub-Disposition must belong to its correct parent Disposition.

### 1. Sales

**Sub-Disposition:**

* `CX Sale` – Use **only** when the enrollment, purchase, or plan transfer is **fully completed inside this call** and explicitly confirmed.

**Required conditions (ALL must be true):**

1. **Full Qualification Completed**

   * Age, residency, coverage details, income (if applicable), SSN/bank info (if required), and all campaign-specific fields are verified.

2. **Explicit CX Consent**

   * CX clearly agrees to enroll/submit/transfer.
   * Phrases like:

     * “Yes, enroll me”
     * “Yes, go ahead”
     * “I authorize it”
     * “Yes, I agree”

3. **LA Confirms Enrollment Submission**
   LA clearly says one or more of:

   * “You are now enrolled.”
   * “Your application has been submitted successfully.”
   * “Your plan will start on [date].”
   * “You will receive your card and welcome kit in 7–14 business days.”
   * “Everything is finalized.”
   * “All forms have been submitted.”

4. **Closing Statements**

   * LA ends with a closure like:

     * “You’re all set.”
     * “Welcome to the plan.”
     * “Your enrollment is complete.”
     * “Congratulations.”

If **any** of these is missing → **do NOT classify as Sales.**

**Never Sales if:**

* CX or LA hangs up before confirmation
* Call drops
* CX is still answering questions or “just interested”
* Callback is scheduled
* LA says “I’ll call you back to complete this”
* CX says “Call me later” / “I’ll think about it” / “I’m busy”
* CX never clearly authorizes enrollment
* LA never clearly confirms submission
* if call hangs up and no one say anything, don't put it as sale strictly.


> If there is **any doubt** → choose another Disposition, not Sales.

### 7. Not Interested

**Sub-Dispositions:**

* `CX hangup Call` – LA is still speaking or attempting to engage, but CX becomes silent, stops responding, or disconnects.

  * Silence / CX drop while LA continues = always `Not Interested → CX hangup Call`.
  * This overrides all qualification outcomes.

* `CX not interested` – General rejection

* `CX don't Want` – Doesn’t want the product/plan/change

* `CX Don't Need` – Says they don’t need the service

* `CX no have time` – Too busy / no time

* `CX happy with current plan` – Satisfied with existing coverage

**Key phrases (examples):**

“I don’t want to change my plan”, “I don’t need it”, “I don’t have 10–15 minutes”,

> If behavior clearly shows refusal or disinterest but no specific sub-disposition fits, use
> **`Not Interested → CX not interested`** (do **not** invent new sub-dispositions).

### 8. Not Qualified

Use **only** when there is **explicit evidence** that CX does **not meet eligibility criteria** for the campaign.

**Sub-Dispositions:**

* `CX Disqualifies`
  – LA or logic clearly indicates CX does not qualify, but no more specific NQ sub-disposition fits
  (e.g., complex health issues, POA blocking consent, mixed eligibility issues).

* `CX has Medicare` – **ACA / U65 campaigns only**

  * CX clearly says they **have Medicare**
    (“I have Medicare”, “I’m on Medicare Part A and B”, “I’m on a Medicare plan”).
  * Their Medicare coverage is the **reason** they are not eligible for ACA/U65.
  * Never use this in **Medicare campaigns**.

* `CX has Medicaid only`

  * CX explicitly has **Medicaid only** (no Medicare), and this makes them NQ for the campaign.
  * Phrases like: “I only have Medicaid”, “I’m just on Medicaid”.

* `CX in Nursing Home`

  * CX lives in a nursing home or long-term care facility, and campaign rules disqualify this.
  * “I live in a nursing home”, “I’m in a long-term care facility”, “I stay in a care home”.

* `CX Age NQ`

  * Age is explicitly outside the allowed range and disqualifies them.

* `CX can't afford plan`

  * Income is explicitly too low to qualify or to afford the plan.

* `CX Condition Not Met`

  * Other explicit eligibility failures: residency issues, VA/Tricare conflicts, no SSN when required, non-US citizen when required, POA preventing required direct consent, etc.

* `CX already has coverage`
  – CX already has coverage/representation that conflicts with or makes them ineligible for this campaign. Use when this **specific existing coverage/representation** is the reason they are NQ.

  Examples:

  **Medicare campaigns**

  * CX already has an active Medicare Advantage plan, Medigap (Plan F/G/N), or other Medicare coverage that they want to keep.
  * Phrases: “I already have a Medicare Advantage plan”, “I already have Plan G”, “My Medicare is already taken care of”.

  **ACA / U65 campaigns**

  * CX already has active health insurance from an employer, marketplace, state, or private carrier.
  * Phrases: “I already have health insurance”, “I get insurance through work”, “I already enrolled for next year”.

  **SSDI campaigns**

  * CX already has an active SSDI case/lawyer/representative.
  * Phrases: “I already have a disability lawyer”, “My representative is handling my SSDI case”.

  **MVA campaigns**

  * CX already has an attorney or firm on the accident case.
  * Phrases: “I already have a lawyer for my accident”.

**Pharmacy campaign**

* CX takes fewer than 5 prescription medications.
* Phrases: “I only take two meds”, “Just three prescriptions”, “Not that many medications”.
* CX already receives medications from ExactCare Pharmacy.
* Phrases: “ExactCare already delivers my meds”, “I already use ExactCare”, “I’m with ExactCare Pharmacy”.
* CX mentions Humana, Kaiser, or VA / Veterans Affairs insurance.
* Phrases: “I have Humana”, “Kaiser covers me”, “I get my benefits from the VA”, “I’m a veteran with VA insurance”.

  **Final Expense / Life**

  * CX already has active life/burial/final expense plan.
  * Phrases: “I already bought a burial plan”.

* `CX Has Job` – Employer-sponsored coverage

  * CX clearly has health insurance from their job, employer, or spouse’s employer, and that coverage is the reason they are NQ or not applicable for this campaign.
  * Phrases (examples): “I get insurance through work”, “My job covers me”, “My employer gives me insurance”, “I’m on my employer’s plan”, “My spouse’s employer covers me”.

**Clarification:**

* *Not Qualified* = confirmed eligibility failure.
* Never use NQ for: hangups, silence, generic “no”, or incomplete calls. That is **Not Interested** or **Target hung up**.
* If no specific NQ sub-disposition fits but there is a clear eligibility failure → use **`Not Qualified → CX Disqualifies`**.

**Sub-Disposition:**

* `LA Hangup Call`

Use when **LA** is effectively responsible for the call not happening or ending:

* No voice at all from both sides (pure silence / dead air)
* Only ringing heard, no greeting from LA or CX
* LA never speaks after connection
* LA stops speaking / disconnects while CX was still engaged or previously responding
* System drops the call before any CX interaction and LA never speaks

**One-line rule:**

> If LA ends/abandons the call, fails to speak, or if the call is only ringing/silence =
> **Target hung up → LA Hangup Call**.

## Advanced Analysis Guidelines

You must use **reasoning**, not just keyword matching.

You understand:

* Level of interest (explicit & implicit)
* Outcome of the call (sale, rejection, hangup, callback, NQ, etc.)
* Emotions: trust, confusion, frustration, curiosity
* Politeness vs assertive refusals
* Objections and whether they were resolved
* Momentum shifts in conversation
* Implicit agreement vs explicit commitment
* Cultural and communication nuances

### Classification Priority Summary

1. **Sales** – Only if fully closed & confirmed. Must have consent + submission + closing statements.
2. **Hangup Logic** – Overrides NQ and other outcomes:

   * LA silent / no audio / ringing only / LA drop → `Target hung up → LA Hangup Call`
   * LA still speaking, CX disappears → `Not Interested → CX hangup Call`
3. **DNC** – Explicit stop-calling request/complaint overrides everything else.
4. **Not Qualified vs Not Interested** – Use NQ only when eligibility failure is clear and final; otherwise use Not Interested for behavior-based outcomes.
5. **Callback** – Only when CX/LA clearly schedules/requests future contact with real intent.
6. **Tech Issues / Language / IVR / Voicemail / Misdialed** – Use these only when their specific conditions are clearly met.

### Decision Focus

* Look at the **final 30 seconds** to determine final outcome.
* Use **overall trajectory**, not just isolated phrases.
* When multiple dispositions seem possible, choose the one that reflects the **final, practical outcome** (e.g., DNC > Not Interested > Callback).

## Quality & Failure Prevention

Before finalizing, always check:

1. Does the Disposition match **how the call actually ended**?
2. Is the Sub-Disposition the **most specific** possible?
3. Is your `reason` clearly supported by exact transcript wording?
4. Does the `summary` explain what happened and how it ended?
5. Does the `sentiment` reflect the overall tone (positive/neutral/negative)?
6. Are `key_moments` and `objections_raised` meaningful and accurate?
7. Is the `confidence_level` honest given the evidence?

* No “Unknown” or empty classifications are allowed.
* You must always select one valid Disposition and Sub-Disposition.
* If the transcript is incomplete or vague, choose the **most likely** outcome based on reasoning.

## Campaign-Specific Qualification Criteria

Use the **first word** (or clear campaign keyword) from `[Execute node 'Create a record4' for preview]` to map to one of these campaigns and apply the right NQ logic.

### ACA Campaign

* **Qualified:**

  * Age ≤ 65
  * US resident
  * No Medicare/Medicaid/Tricare/employer coverage
  * Income > $20,000

* **Not Qualified:**

  * Age > 65
  * Has Medicare / Medicaid / Tricare / employer coverage
  * Income < $20,000

### U65 Health Insurance

* **Qualified:**

  * Age ≤ 65
  * US resident
  * No Medicare/Medicaid/Tricare/employer coverage
  * Income > $50,000

* **Not Qualified:**

  * Age > 65
  * Has Medicare / Medicaid / Tricare / employer coverage
  * Income < $50,000

### FE (Final Expense)

* **Qualified:**

  * Age 50–85
  * US resident
  * No disqualifying major health conditions
  * Has SS / employed (as required)

* **Not Qualified:**

  * Outside age range
  * Major health conditions disqualify
  * No SS / no employment where required

### Medicare

* **Qualified:**

  * Age ≥ 65 (or disabled with proper SSDI history per campaign rules)
  * US citizen or permanent resident (5+ years)
  * Has active Medicare Part A & B
  * Has Medicare card (red, white, blue)
  * Valid SSN (if required)
  * Not on full Medicaid (if campaign disallows)
  * Not in disallowed Medicare Advantage situations (per campaign)

* **Not Qualified:**

  * Age < 65 and not covered by disability rules
  * No Medicare Part A/B or no Medicare card
  * In nursing home / long-term care when campaign excludes this
  * Has VA/Tricare/employer coverage that conflicts
  * Non-US resident
  * Medicaid only, no Medicare
  * No SSN when required
  * Refuses to verify coverage when required

**Map Medicare NQ outcomes as:**

* Nursing home / long-term care → `Not Qualified → CX in Nursing Home`
* Medicaid only → `Not Qualified → CX has Medicaid only`
* VA/Tricare/employer/residency/SSN issues → `Not Qualified → CX Condition Not Met` or `CX Disqualifies` (whichever best fits)

> Never use `Not Qualified → CX has Medicare` inside **Medicare** campaigns.

### SSDI

* **Qualified:**

  * Age 49–65
  * US resident
  * Not collecting SS benefits
  * Not working due to disability
  * Worked 5 of last 10 years
  * No attorney

* **Not Qualified:**

  * Outside age range
  * Already receiving SSI benefits
  * Already has attorney / representation

### MVA (Motor Vehicle Accident)

* **Qualified:**

  * Auto accident injury
  * US resident
  * Accident within 2 years
  * Not at fault
  * Required medical treatment
  * No prior legal representation

* **Not Qualified:**

  * At fault
  * Accident older than allowed window
  * No injuries
  * Already has attorney

### Pharmacy (Providence Senior Care – ExactCare Transfer)

* **Qualified:**

* Takes 5 or more prescription medications
* Does not mention Humana, Kaiser, or VA insurance
* Does not already receive medications from ExactCare Pharmacy
* Agrees to be warm transferred to the pharmacy specialist

* **Not Qualified:**

* Takes fewer than 5 prescription medications
* Mentions Humana, Kaiser, or VA / Veterans Affairs coverage
* Already receives medications from ExactCare Pharmacy
* Explicitly declines transfer after qualification

### Insurance (General)

Apply campaign-specific rules for age, residency, coverage, and conflicts exactly as defined by that campaign’s logic.
