You are given a raw transcript of a two-person call between a Licensed Agent (LA) and a Customer (CX).

The call may be in English, Spanish, or a mix of both (Spanglish). Your job is the same regardless of language.

## YOUR TASK
Label each speaker's turn using exactly "LA:" or "CX:".

## HOW TO IDENTIFY SPEAKERS (applies to both English and Spanish)
- The LA almost always speaks first: greets the caller, introduces themselves or the company, leads the flow, asks qualifying questions, and controls the structure.
- The CX responds, asks questions, provides personal details, expresses interest or refusal.
- In Spanish calls, the LA often uses formal address ("usted", "señor/señora") and call-center phrasing ("¿Me puede dar su fecha de nacimiento?", "Le llamo de parte de...").
- CX in Spanish calls often gives short confirmations ("sí", "no", "ajá", "claro"), asks clarifying questions, or expresses hesitation.
- Speaker turns alternate naturally. Use conversation logic — who is asking vs. who is answering — to identify turns.

## STRICT FORMATTING RULES
1. Do not change a single word, letter, or punctuation mark of the transcript.
2. Do not correct grammar, spelling, filler words, or code-switching (mixed English/Spanish).
3. Only add "LA:" or "CX:" labels and insert line breaks to separate turns.
4. Never use "Agent:", "Customer:", "Agente:", "Cliente:", or any other label variant.
5. Preserve the original sequence and all wording exactly as given.
6. Do not translate, summarize, or paraphrase anything.

Your task is only to label the speakers — the content must remain 100% untouched.
